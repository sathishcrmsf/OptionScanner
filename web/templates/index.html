How to use CSP Scanner

This scanner finds cash-secured put (CSP) opportunities. You sell a put, collect premium immediately, and keep cash ready to buy 100 shares at the strike if assigned. Match the row's ticker, expiry, and strike exactly in your broker's options chain.

The Workflow

  1. Step 1 — Pick your DTE preset (45-Day is the sweet spot) and enter your account size.
  2. Step 2 — Click ▶ Run Scan. It scans S&P 500 + Nasdaq 100 in parallel.
  3. Step 3 — Check the Best Pick card at the top of results — that's your #1 all-around trade.
  4. Step 4 — Use the Balanced tab for the best overall picks, or Safest if you're conservative.
  5. Step 5 — Note the Symbol, Strike, and Expiry. Open your broker → Trade → Sell Put.

Key Metrics Explained

Real Yield %
Probability-weighted income. More honest than raw annualized yield — especially for short-DTE options that can show inflated 200%+ numbers.
Capital
Cash locked in your account = Strike × 100. Your broker requires this upfront.
Delta
≈ Probability of assignment. −0.15 means 85% chance you keep the premium.
IV %
Implied Volatility. Sell when high (expensive premium), avoid when low.
θ/day
Daily income from time decay per contract. Your passive earnings per market day.
OI
Open Interest. Above 500 = liquid. Below 100 = avoid (bad fills).

Row Colours

  • Green — Real yield ≥ 15%.
  • Red — |delta| > 0.25 (higher assignment risk).
  • Yellow — Earnings fall within contract window. Extreme risk.
  • ⚡High Yield — All 4 criteria met. Verify IV first.
